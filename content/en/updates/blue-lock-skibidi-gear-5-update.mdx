With the power creep introduced in the **blue lock skibidi gear 5 update**, playing as a Goalkeeper (GK) has become more challenging. Characters like Shidou and Gear 5 Luffy can release shots from angles that were previously impossible to cover. To succeed, you must master the Q-dive mechanic and maintain perfect positioning.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Maintain Central Positioning">
    Always start in the center of the goal. Adjust your position slightly toward the near post as the attacker approaches from the wing, but never leave the far post completely unguarded.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Track the Ball, Not the Player">
    Many high-tier strikers use feints or dribble moves (Q) to bait a dive. Wait until the ball actually leaves the player's foot or the ability animation triggers before committing to a dive.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Master the Q-Dive Timing">
    Pressing Q too early leaves you vulnerable to a simple tap-in. Pressing it too late means you won't reach the corners. Practice the timing against standard shots before trying to block Secret-rarity abilities.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute Controlled Clearances">
    Once you catch or block the ball, use the Left Mouse Button to clear it. Aim for an open teammate in the midfield or kick it toward the sidelines to reset the opponent's pressure.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Communicate with Defenders">
    If a defender is already covering the near post, you can focus more on the center. Use the "Request Ball" (R) signal if you want your defenders to pass back to you for a safe reset.
  </Step>
</Steps>
